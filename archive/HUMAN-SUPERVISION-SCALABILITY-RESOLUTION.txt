HUMAN-SUPERVISION-SCALABILITY-RESOLUTION.txt
=========================================

ADDRESSES
---------
Human Supervision Scalability (see SYSTEMIC-GOVERNANCE-PROBLEMS.txt)

PURPOSE
-------
This document defines a minimal, concrete, and technology-agnostic method
to resolve **Problem #8: Scalability of Human Supervision** in distributed,
automated, or AI-augmented systems.

This approach complements BISS-RIG.
It does NOT modify BISS-RIG.
It reduces cognitive load without reducing observability.


PROBLEM STATEMENT
-----------------
As systems scale, even when everything is:
- correctly executed
- causally explained
- state-declared
- quality-measured
- globally visible

humans still fail to supervise effectively because:
- too many events
- too many alerts
- too many metrics
- too many correlations

This results in:
- alert fatigue
- ignored signals
- delayed reactions
- loss of effective control

This is not a correctness problem.
It is a **human cognitive capacity problem**.


CORE PRINCIPLE
--------------
Humans must supervise **summaries**, not raw data.

Supervision must:
- reduce volume
- preserve meaning
- surface only what requires attention

It must never:
- hide facts
- discard evidence
- replace raw data
- make decisions automatically


MINIMAL SOLUTION
----------------
Introduce a **Supervision Synthesis Layer** that produces
human-oriented summaries derived from existing data.

This layer is:
- read-only
- non-authoritative
- discardable


SUPERVISION ARTIFACTS
---------------------
The layer produces synthesized supervision artifacts such as:

- health summaries
- anomaly digests
- risk rankings
- change summaries
- attention queues

These artifacts are outputs only.


SYNTHESIS RULES
---------------

Rule 1: Synthesis is lossy but traceable
- Summaries may omit detail
- Every summary must link back to raw data

Rule 2: Focus on deviation
- Normal behavior is compressed
- Change and deviation are expanded

Rule 3: Time windows are explicit
- Summaries are always bounded in time

Rule 4: No automatic escalation
- The layer flags attention
- Humans decide action


EXAMPLE SUPERVISION SUMMARY
---------------------------

Time window: last 24 hours

Summary:
- 1 new critical degradation detected
- 3 workflows show increased latency
- 1 reasoning version correlates with quality drop
- No execution failures detected

Attention queue:
1. CORR-33 – latency degradation trend
2. CORR-12 – reasoning version v2025-01 drift


WHAT THIS ENABLES
-----------------
With supervision synthesis, operators can:

- Maintain situational awareness
- Focus on anomalies instead of noise
- React earlier and more accurately
- Scale oversight without scaling staff

Example questions now answerable:
- What changed since yesterday?
- Where should attention be focused now?
- Is the system stable or drifting?
- Are we missing something important?


WHAT THIS DOES NOT DO
--------------------
This mechanism does NOT:
- suppress raw logs
- hide failures
- auto-remediate
- enforce policies
- replace human judgment

Raw data always remains accessible.


MENTAL MODEL
------------
Raw data is infinite.
Human attention is finite.

Supervision synthesis bridges that gap.


RELATION TO BISS-RIG
-------------------
- BISS-RIG provides reliable execution facts
- Supervision synthesis makes those facts usable by humans

They must remain strictly separated.


SUCCESS CRITERIA
----------------
Problem #8 is resolved if and only if:

- Humans can understand system state in minutes, not hours
- Attention is directed to deviations, not volume
- Raw evidence remains available
- Removing the synthesis layer does not affect execution


FINAL NOTE
----------
Most system failures are not caused by lack of data.
They are caused by too much data.

Scalable human supervision requires reduction,
not simplification of truth.
