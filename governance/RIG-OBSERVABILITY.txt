RIG – Observability and Diagnostics
===================================

This document is SUPPORTING.
It defines how observability, logs, debug output, and test diagnostics
fit into the RIG model.

------------------------------------------------------------

1. OBSERVABILITY
------------------------------------------------------------

RIG treats logs, debug output, traces, and test diagnostics as observables
that support reasoning about behavior.

These signals are:
- descriptive, not authoritative
- structured when possible
- attributable to the interaction, step, or object they explain

------------------------------------------------------------

2. SIGNAL TYPES
------------------------------------------------------------

- log: stable informational signal for operators and automation
- debug: high-volume diagnostic signal for local investigation
- trace: causal linkage across steps or components
- test diagnostic: assertion-context output used by TIC or test runners

None of these signals may replace the contract result.

------------------------------------------------------------

3. META-OBSERVATION
------------------------------------------------------------

When the logging or debug pipeline itself is the subject of inspection,
that is meta-observation rather than ordinary observation.

------------------------------------------------------------
